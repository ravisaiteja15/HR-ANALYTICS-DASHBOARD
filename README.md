# 💼 HR-ANALYTICS-DASHBOARD

### 📌Project Overview
Developed an HR Attrition Analytics Dashboard in Power BI to analyze employee turnover patterns and identify factors influencing attrition. Created interactive visualizations and KPIs to analyze attrition trends based on department, job role, salary, age, gender, and experience. Applied data cleaning, data modeling, and DAX calculations to generate actionable workforce insights.

---

### 🚀Objectives
- Analyze employee demographics
- Study employee attrition patterns
- Identify key factors influencing employee turnover
- Visualize HR metrics using charts and dashboards
- Build predictive models for employee attrition

---

### 🛠️ Tools & Technologies Used
- 📊 Power BI
- 🧹 Power Query (Data Cleaning & Transformation)
- 📐 DAX (Data Analysis Expressions)
- 📁 Excel / CSV files
- 📈 Data Visualization

---

### 📂Dataset Information
- **Source :** Kaggle HR Analytics Dataset
- Contains employee-related information covering demographics, job roles, salary details, work experience, performance, satisfaction levels, and attrition status.
 - Provides data points to identify factors influencing employee turnover and workforce trends.

---
 ### 📚KPI Insights
- Attrition Rate: 16.3%
- Highest Attrition Department: Administration (36%)
- Highest Employee Count: Operations (512)
- Most Employees Age Group: 26–35 Years
- Higher Attrition among Male Employees (63.2%)
- Attrition is highest for employees with 0–5 years of experience

### 🎯Conclusion
- Sales department records the highest employee attrition, making it the primary area of concern.
- The 26–35 age group forms the largest share of the workforce.
- Attrition is highest among employees with lower experience, indicating early-career retention challenges.
- Male employees have a higher attrition count than female employees.
- Employees in the 6–10 LPA salary range show the highest attrition.
- Operations is the largest department by employee count, while Finance has the smallest workforce.
